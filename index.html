about

I'm a graduate researcher at North Dakota State University working at the intersection of natural language processing and genomic sequence modeling — applying the architectures built for human language directly to DNA and protein sequences. That means custom tokenizers for biological sequences, pre-training and fine-tuning foundation models, and modifying attention mechanisms so they attend to the things that matter in a protein or a strand of DNA.

Before graduate school, I spent three years as a backend engineer leading feature delivery and cloud infrastructure for a remote team — experience that now shows up as pipelines that actually scale, not just notebooks that run once. I'm looking for a computational biology, bioinformatics, or applied ML role where that combination — production engineering discipline plus foundation-model research — is the point, not the exception.

Manuscript submitted
UBLM: A Unified Bio-Language Model

A foundation model that jointly represents DNA and protein sequences in one shared vocabulary of nucleotide triplets and amino acids, pre-trained on 277M+ sequences from representative E. coli strains and mapped into a shared embedding space. Fine-tuned into two applications: UBLM-Gene-Finder, which outperforms Prodigal and GeneMarkS-2 at identifying gene boundaries — particularly on short genes — and UBLM-Optimizer, which generates coding sequences that outperform CodonTransformer and ICOR on naturalness and viability. The first unified foundation model to jointly model DNA and protein in E. coli, laying groundwork for future models spanning RNA, carbohydrates, and metabolites in more complex organisms.
